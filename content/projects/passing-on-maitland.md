## The Brief

Funded by the NSW Government's Streets as Shared Spaces program, Maitland City Council commissioned InkHunter to create the inaugural artwork for their ambitious Maitlanes project — transforming seven underutilised laneways into vibrant public spaces through art and placemaking.

## Community Engagement

Working with the Maitland community, InkHunter explored what the local environment meant to residents — the Hunter River, the native eucalyptus forests, and the delicate balance between development and preservation that defines regional NSW towns.

## Process

"Passing On" centres on themes of environmental impact and human legacy. A native tree grows from the composition, its gum leaves flowing outward into rippling waves. Within the organic forms, a fingerprint and footprint emerge — the marks we leave on the world, both seen and unseen.

The work was completed entirely with Colormaker sustainable paints, manufactured with 100% renewable energy — a first for the Maitlanes project and a standard InkHunter has maintained since.

## Outcome

"Passing On" set the creative benchmark for the six laneways that followed, demonstrating how a single artwork could transform a forgotten corridor into a destination. The laneway saw a measurable increase in pedestrian use and became a conversation starter about environmental responsibility in the Hunter Valley.
